 # Is ADHD a Disability?
 
 Is ADHD a disability? Yes. ADHD is recognized as a disability under the ADA, Section 504, and other laws. But what that means in practice -- at work, at school, and for benefits -- depends on your situation. Here is what you need to know.
 
 ## Where does ADHD qualify as a disability?
 
 - **Under the ADA:** The Americans with Disabilities Act protects individuals with ADHD in employment, public services, and daily life when ADHD substantially limits a major life activity.
 - **At School:** Section 504 plans, IEPs, and your child's rights to accommodations in K-12 and college.
 - **In the Workplace:** Reasonable accommodations, disclosure strategies, and protection against discrimination.
 - **For SSDI Benefits:** How to apply for Social Security Disability benefits when ADHD limits your ability to work.
 
 ## Key legal frameworks at a glance
 
 | Law | Who It Protects | ADHD Coverage |
 |---|---|---|
 | ADA | Employees, public access | Yes, if ADHD substantially limits a major life activity |
 | Section 504 | Students in public schools | Yes, qualifies for 504 Plan accommodations |
 | IDEA | Students needing special education | Yes, under "Other Health Impairment" category |
 | SSDI / SSI | Workers unable to work | Possible, but high bar for ADHD alone |
 
 ## How to get ADHD recognized as a disability
 
 1. **Get a formal diagnosis:** Obtain a documented ADHD diagnosis from a licensed psychologist, psychiatrist, or physician. This is the foundation for all disability claims.
 2. **Document functional limitations:** Work with your doctor to document exactly how ADHD limits your daily functioning -- at work, in school, or in other major life activities.
 3. **Identify the right legal framework:** Determine which law applies: ADA for employment, Section 504 or IDEA for education, or SSDI for benefits.
 4. **Submit your request:** For workplace accommodations, submit a formal request to HR. For school, request a 504 evaluation in writing. For SSDI, file through the SSA.
 
 ## Frequently asked questions
 
 **Is ADHD a disability under the ADA?**
 
 Yes. ADHD can qualify as a disability under the Americans with Disabilities Act when it substantially limits one or more major life activities. The ADA Amendments Act of 2008 broadened the definition to make it easier for conditions like ADHD to qualify.
 
 **Can I get SSDI benefits for ADHD?**
 
 It is possible but challenging. The SSA requires proof that ADHD severely limits your ability to perform work-related activities. Most successful claims involve ADHD combined with other conditions. Strong medical documentation is essential.
 
 **Is ADHD a learning disability?**
 
 ADHD is not technically classified as a specific learning disability (like dyslexia or dyscalculia). However, it frequently co-occurs with learning disabilities and is recognized as a disability under Section 504 and the ADA, meaning students with ADHD are entitled to accommodations.
 
 **What accommodations can I get for ADHD?**
 
 Common workplace accommodations include flexible schedules, noise-reducing headphones, written instructions, frequent breaks, and task management tools. School accommodations may include extended test time, preferential seating, and modified assignments.
