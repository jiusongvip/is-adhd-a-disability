 # Is ADHD a Disability in School?
 
 Yes. ADHD is recognized as a disability in school settings under Section 504 and, in many cases, under IDEA. Students with ADHD are entitled to accommodations and support. Here is how it works.
 
 ## Two paths: Section 504 vs. IDEA
 
 There are two main federal laws that require public schools to support students with ADHD:
 
 | Feature | Section 504 Plan | IEP (Under IDEA) |
 |---|---|---|
 | Eligibility | Any student with a disability that affects a major life activity | ADHD must adversely affect educational performance and require special education |
 | Scope | Accommodations and services to ensure equal access | Specialized instruction, goals, services, and accommodations |
 | Evaluation | School evaluates or reviews existing records | Comprehensive evaluation required |
 | Parent involvement | School must notify parents of decisions | Parents are part of the IEP team |
 | Common for ADHD | Most common -- simpler process | Used when ADHD significantly impacts learning |
 
 ## Common 504 Plan accommodations for ADHD
 
 A Section 504 Plan is the most common way students with ADHD receive support:
 
 - **Extended time** on tests and assignments
 - **Preferential seating** near the teacher, away from distractions
 - **Breaking assignments** into smaller chunks
 - **Frequent breaks** during long tasks
 - **Written instructions** in addition to verbal directions
 - **Noise-reducing headphones** during independent work
 - **Check-ins** with the teacher to ensure understanding
 - **Modified homework** -- reduced volume, not reduced difficulty
 
 ## IDEA and the "Other Health Impairment" category
 
 Under IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act), ADHD falls under the **"Other Health Impairment" (OHI)** category. For an IEP, the student must demonstrate that ADHD **adversely affects educational performance** to the point where specialized instruction is needed -- not just accommodations.
 
 Many students with ADHD can manage with a 504 Plan. An IEP is appropriate when ADHD causes significant academic difficulties that require specialized teaching methods, behavior intervention plans, or related services like counseling or occupational therapy.
 
 ## How to request a 504 evaluation
 
 1. **Write a formal request** to your child's school principal or 504 coordinator. The request should be in writing and dated.
 2. **Include documentation** of ADHD diagnosis from a healthcare provider.
 3. **Describe specific challenges** your child faces at school due to ADHD.
 4. **The school must respond** -- they are legally required to evaluate if they suspect a disability.
 5. **Attend the evaluation meeting** -- you have the right to be present.
 6. **If eligible, work with the team** to develop the 504 Plan.
 
 ## College and post-secondary education
 
 In college, the dynamic shifts. IDEA no longer applies, and Section 504 and the ADA govern disability services:
 
 - **The student must self-identify** and request accommodations through the disability services office
 - **Colleges are not required to provide IEPs** -- they provide "academic adjustments" under Section 504
 - **Documentation requirements** vary by institution
 - **Common college accommodations:** extended test time, note-taking services, priority registration, reduced course load
 
 **Disclaimer:** This page provides general information about educational rights for students with ADHD. Procedures and rights vary by state and school district. Contact your local school district's special education office or an education advocate for specific guidance.
