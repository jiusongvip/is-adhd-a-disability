 # Is ADHD a Disability in Your Country?
 
 ADHD disability status varies by country. Here is how major English-speaking countries and the European Union classify ADHD under their disability laws.
 
 ## United States
 
 **Laws:** ADA, Section 504, IDEA, Fair Housing Act
 
 Yes -- ADHD is recognized as a disability under multiple federal laws when it substantially limits major life activities. SSDI/SSI benefits possible but difficult. Workplace and education accommodations available. Most comprehensive protections globally.
 
 ## United Kingdom
 
 **Law:** Equality Act 2010
 
 Yes -- ADHD can qualify as a disability if it has a substantial and long-term adverse effect on daily activities. Employers must make reasonable adjustments. Access to Work scheme provides funding for workplace support. PIP (Personal Independence Payment) available.
 
 ## Canada
 
 **Laws:** Canadian Human Rights Act, Provincial Codes
 
 Yes -- ADHD is recognized as a disability under federal and provincial human rights legislation. Duty to accommodate applies across employment, education, and services. CPP Disability benefits may be available in some cases.
 
 ## Australia
 
 **Law:** Disability Discrimination Act 1992
 
 Yes -- ADHD is covered under disability discrimination law. NDIS (National Disability Insurance Scheme) access possible with proper documentation. Workplace and education accommodations available.
 
 ## European Union
 
 **Laws:** EU Employment Equality Directive, UN CRPD
 
 Varies by country, but generally yes under EU directives. Most EU countries recognize ADHD as a disability for employment nondiscrimination. Individual country implementations differ significantly.
 
 ## Ireland
 
 **Laws:** Employment Equality Acts, Equal Status Acts
 
 Yes -- ADHD is recognized as a disability under Irish equality legislation. Reasonable accommodation requirement applies in employment and education.
 
 ## New Zealand
 
 **Laws:** Human Rights Act 1993, Health and Disability Commissioner Act
 
 Yes -- ADHD is covered under disability discrimination protections. Reasonable accommodation obligations in employment and education.
 
 **Disclaimer:** This comparison is a general overview. Laws change and interpretations vary. Consult a local disability advocate or attorney for current guidance in your jurisdiction.
