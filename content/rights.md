 # ADHD Disability Rights Checklist
 
 A clear, practical overview of your legal rights under US disability laws. Know what protections apply to you, across employment, education, public services, and housing.
 
 ## Employment (ADA)
 
 - Right to reasonable accommodations
 - Protection from discrimination
 - Confidentiality of medical information
 - Right to participate in the interactive process
 - Right to file an EEOC complaint
 
 ## Education K-12 (Section 504/IDEA)
 
 - Free appropriate public education (FAPE)
 - Evaluation for disability at no cost to parents
 - 504 Plan or IEP with individualized accommodations
 - Least restrictive environment placement
 - Procedural safeguards for parents
 
 ## Higher Education (ADA/Section 504)
 
 - Academic adjustments and auxiliary aids
 - Access to campus facilities and programs
 - Right to self-identify and request accommodations
 - Testing accommodations (extended time, separate room)
 - Note-taking and recording lecture permissions
 
 ## Public Services (ADA Title II)
 
 - Equal access to government services and programs
 - Reasonable modifications to policies and procedures
 - Effective communication (auxiliary aids)
 - Accessible public transportation
 
 ## Housing (Fair Housing Act)
 
 - Reasonable accommodations in housing rules and policies
 - Reasonable modifications to living space
 - Protection from disability-based discrimination in renting and buying
 
 ## What to do if your rights are violated
 
 1. **Document the incident.** Write down what happened, when, who was involved, and any witnesses.
 2. **File an internal complaint** with your employer, school, or service provider.
 3. **File a complaint with the relevant agency** (EEOC for employment, OCR for education, HUD for housing).
 4. **Consult an attorney** who specializes in disability law.
 
 **Disclaimer:** This checklist is a general overview and does not constitute legal advice. Rights vary by jurisdiction and individual circumstances.
