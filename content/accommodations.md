 # ADHD Workplace Accommodations: A Complete Guide
 
 Reasonable accommodations are adjustments to your work environment that help you perform your job effectively. Here is a detailed list of accommodations that can help people with ADHD thrive at work.
 
 ## Focus and distraction management
 
 - Private office or quiet workspace away from high-traffic areas
 - Noise-canceling headphones or white noise machine
 - Permission to use focus apps that block distracting websites
 - "Do not disturb" blocks on calendar for deep work periods
 - Reduced visual distractions (desk away from windows, cubicle walls)
 
 ## Time management and organization
 
 - Flexible schedule with adjusted start/end times
 - Remote or hybrid work options
 - Regular one-on-one check-ins with supervisor to clarify priorities
 - Written instructions for tasks, not just verbal
 - Project management software with clear deadlines and reminders
 - Breaking large projects into smaller milestones
 - Additional time for learning new systems or processes
 
 ## Communication support
 
 - Written summaries after meetings
 - Clear, specific feedback with actionable steps
 - Email templates for routine communications
 - Alternative communication channels (e.g., Slack over phone calls)
 - Grace period to formulate responses in meetings
 
 ## Technology and tools
 
 - Speech-to-text software for note-taking
 - Dual monitors to reduce window-switching
 - Digital task management apps (Todoist, Notion, Asana)
 - Calendar software with customizable reminders
 - Grammarly or similar writing assistance tools
 - Browser extensions to limit tab overload
 
 ## Work environment adjustments
 
 - Standing desk or treadmill desk option
 - Fidget tools (stress ball, fidget cube) permitted
 - Permission to take short movement breaks
 - Natural lighting where possible (reduced fluorescent light exposure)
 - Temperature control within reasonable limits
 
 ## How to request accommodations
 
 1. **Identify the specific need:** What exactly is challenging, and what adjustment would help?
 2. **Document your diagnosis:** Get a letter from your healthcare provider describing your ADHD and recommended accommodations.
 3. **Submit a written request:** Address it to HR or your disability services coordinator.
 4. **Be specific but flexible:** Propose accommodations you think would help, but be open to alternatives.
 5. **Participate in the interactive process:** Work with your employer to find solutions that work for both sides.
 
 **Disclaimer:** This page provides general information about accommodations. The specific accommodations that are reasonable vary by job, employer, and individual circumstances.
